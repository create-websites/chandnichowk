The Imperial Blueprint

A City by Design

Shahjahanabad, a 17th-century marvel, was essentially a "smart city." It boasted a walled 1,500-acre area with 14 gates, designed with broad streets and a cutting-edge canal system, the *Nahr-i-Behisht*, drawing water from the Yamuna, reflecting Mughal urban brilliance.

Chandni Chowk, planned by Princess Jahanara Begum, became the vibrant core of the city, a grand ceremonial and commercial boulevard.
