Chandni Chowk

Crafted in 1650 by Princess Jahanara, this space, named "Moonlight Square," once boasted a reflecting pool and canal. Celebrated for silver vendors, it morphed into specialized markets. Now, it's a sprawling, bustling wholesale market, a major Asian hub.

Khari Baoli

Established in 1850, it reigns as Asia's biggest spice hub. The space brims with the strong smells of spices such as chilies, turmeric, and cardamom, and offers one of Old Delhi's most captivating sensory treats.

Chawri Bazaar

Founded in 1840, Delhi's original wholesale market focused on hardware. It evolved into a key hub for brass, copper, and paper goods, notably wedding stationery.

Paranthe Wali Gali

Since the 1870s, this "paratha lane" has flourished. These vegetarian eateries fry distinct *parathas*, filled with unusual ingredients such as cashew, mint, and sweet *khoya*. A landmark, it charmed figures like Nehru.

Old vs. New: A Tale of Two Cities

Clash of Ideologies

Shahjahanabad intertwined ruler & city via public display. Lutyens' Delhi prioritized separation and control, an administrative space distanced from the old city. This creation of a "New" Delhi deliberately positioned Mughal Delhi as outdated and historical.

The Conservation Conundrum

Old Delhi grapples with congestion and illicit building, pitting preservation against practical needs. Historic *havelis* become storage spaces, while top-down "beautification" of areas like Chandni Chowk often falters, neglecting the district's unique dynamism and hurting residents' businesses.
